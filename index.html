Starbucks Executive Team Charter Canvas
Clarity, Trust, Accountability, Performance, Growth
Purpose & Values
Unified team vision and purpose, core values (Curiosity, Responsibility, Care, Customer focus), and commitment to tie all decisions and actions to this charter.
Panel: Make the purpose statement a “living” section—review and refresh quarterly as the team and context evolve.
Roles & Expectations
Explicit definition of team member roles, responsibility map, mutual expectations, and active ownership of both team and cross-team goals.
Panel: Set up a regular cadence (e.g. kickoff of each cycle/quarter) to clarify and renew team and role expectations.
Trust & Psychological Safety
No-surprises transparency, constructive challenge as a norm, and explicit peer support in high-stakes or difficult conversations.
Panel: Use short pulse surveys and health checks monthly to assure trust and surface safety concerns early.
Accountability
Team-owned metrics, public commitments, regular follow-up, blameless correction of slip-ups, and a focus on collective learning and improvement.
Panel: Separate “accountability to outcomes” from “accountability to team behaviors.” Treat misses as opportunities to learn together.
Collaboration & Feedback
Cross-functional workshops, scheduled reflections, peer-to-peer and group feedback sessions, with open invitation to coach or mentor.
Panel: Embed a lightweight review (e.g. quick learning review post-milestone, deep quarterly retrospective) into the rhythm.
Decision-Making
Agreed criteria for decision rights, documented decisions, transparent escalation, and rapid iterative experimentation when stakes are uncertain.
Panel: Define which decisions require consensus and which can be made by delegated roles. Periodically review these guidelines as the team matures.
Canvas created with Starbucks ELT and expert panel, following Black House maturity principles.
